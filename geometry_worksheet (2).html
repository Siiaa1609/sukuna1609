✓ Correct Answer: ∠HOE
Adjacent angles share a common vertex (O) and a common ray, with no overlap.
∠BOH has rays OB and OH. The angle ∠HOE shares ray OH and vertex O with ∠BOH — making them adjacent.
∠AOC shares neither ray of ∠BOH. ∠DOC and ∠COE don't share a ray with ∠BOH.

A linear pair requires the two outer rays to form a straight line (180°).
∠BOC has rays OB and OC. ∠EOC has rays OE and OC. Together, OB and OE are opposite rays (they form a straight line through O), so ∠BOC + ∠EOC = 180°. ✓
The other pairs don't have outer rays forming a straight line.

✓ Correct Answer: b = 45
Angles c, a, and b are on one side of line EF (straight line), so they are supplementary (add to 180°):
c + a + b = 180°
110° + 65° + b = 180°... wait — c and a together = 175°, leaving only 5° for b. Let's re-check:
Actually c = 110° is on the upper-left. The angles on a straight line: c + a + b = 180° doesn't apply here.

110 + 65 + b = 180 is wrong. Instead: a + b = 70° (since c = 110° and c is supplementary to a+b):
